# Summit Scene Legal Drafts

Last updated: May 4, 2026

These drafts are working product/legal copy for testing and launch preparation.
They are not legal advice. Before publishing Summit Scene publicly, have a
qualified lawyer review the final Privacy Policy, Terms of Use, app store
privacy labels, and local compliance language.

## Documents To Publish

1. Privacy Policy
   - Must be available inside the app.
   - Must also be available at a public, non-PDF URL for app store metadata.
   - Should name the developer/company, privacy contact, data collected, data
     uses, service providers, retention/deletion, consent withdrawal, and account
     deletion.

2. Terms of Use
   - Should cover account rules, acceptable use, user content, business account
     authorization, moderation, disclaimers, and limits of responsibility.

3. Community Guidelines
   - Should cover respectful use, safety, reporting/blocking, prohibited content,
     scams, harassment, misleading event details, and public-meetup safety.

4. Local Event and Business Compliance Notice
   - Should tell businesses and organizers that they are responsible for required
     permits, licences, insurance, venue approvals, food/alcohol approvals, road
     use approvals, Parks Canada requirements, and municipal bylaws.

## Privacy Policy Draft

Summit Scene collects information needed to create accounts, show local events,
support community features, review business accounts, moderate reports, and keep
the app working.

Data collected may include:

- Account information: name or business name, email, password hash, role, town,
  profile details, avatar, social links, and business verification status.
- Public content: event posts, community posts, buddy posts, replies, profile
  information, likes, interests, attendance choices, and saved event choices.
- Optional location: device location for nearby event features after permission
  is granted. Event addresses may be sent to address lookup/geocoding services
  to place events on the map.
- Safety and moderation data: reports, blocks, moderation notes, and admin review
  actions.
- Technical and support data: request logs, errors, support emails, and bug
  reports.

Data may be processed by service providers used for hosting, database storage,
address lookup/geocoding, social login, and AI event description generation.

Users can delete their account in the Account screen. Account deletion removes
the account and associated app data, except where limited retention is required
for security, abuse prevention, dispute handling, or legal compliance.

Privacy contact: hello@summitscene.ca

## Terms Of Use Draft

By using Summit Scene, users agree to use the app lawfully, respectfully, and
honestly.

- Users must provide accurate account information.
- Users must not impersonate another person, business, venue, organization, or
  event organizer.
- Business and organizer accounts must only be created by people authorized to
  represent that business or organization.
- Users are responsible for the content they post.
- Users must not post harassment, threats, scams, spam, misleading event details,
  unsafe instructions, illegal content, or content that violates another person's
  rights.
- Summit Scene may remove content, restrict accounts, reject business
  verification, or take moderation action when needed for safety, compliance, or
  app integrity.
- Summit Scene is an events and community discovery tool. It is not an emergency
  service, government service, municipal authority, Parks Canada authority,
  travel advisor, or permit/licensing advisor.

## Local Compliance Notice Draft

Businesses and organizers are responsible for confirming and following all
permits, licences, insurance, park rules, municipal bylaws, venue rules, alcohol
rules, food rules, road use rules, and safety requirements that apply to their
event or business activity.

Posting or approving an event in Summit Scene does not mean the event is
licensed, permitted, insured, or approved by Parks Canada, the Town of Banff, the
Town of Canmore, Alberta Health Services, AGLC, or any venue.

## Launch Checklist

- Add public web URLs for Privacy Policy and account deletion requests.
- Add privacy links in App Store Connect and Google Play Console.
- Complete Apple privacy details and Google Play Data safety form.
- Keep in-app Privacy & Terms accessible from logged-out and logged-in screens.
- Keep the in-app account deletion button working.
- Add email verification before public launch.
- Add marketing email opt-in before sending newsletters or promotional emails.
- Review event posting UI for local permit reminders.
- Have final policies reviewed by counsel before production launch.
